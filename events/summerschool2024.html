Summer School 2024: Hands On Astronomy at F.E.L.O

“Something as mere as age in the immensity of Curiosity, is but a young Sun against the entirety of the Sky.”

Hands-on Astronomy at F.E.L.O 2024, organised by the Postgraduate and Research Department of Physics in association with the Xaverian Astronomical Society, was the concluding societal event of XAS for the academic session 2023–24. The two-day summer workshop was designed for students of Classes XI and XII from St. Xavier’s Collegiate School and Don Bosco School, Bandel.

Conducted on 19th and 20th July, the workshop combined theoretical discussions, hands-on activities at the Fr. Eugene Lafont Observatory, and computational analysis of experimental data, with the aim of instilling curiosity and appreciation for Astronomy and Astrophysics among young learners.

Inauguration and Foundational Concepts

The workshop was inaugurated on 19th July at D’Souza Hall, Room 15, by Rev. Dr. Dominic Savio SJ, Principal of St. Xavier’s College (Autonomous), Kolkata. This was followed by the felicitation of the speaker, Dr. Sanjay Kumar Ghosh, retired professor from Bose Institute, Kolkata.

An address by Dr. Shibaji Banerjee, Head of the Department of Physics, followed the inaugural ceremony. After a short tea break, current students and alumni of the Department of Physics conducted a video presentation and student session introducing foundational concepts such as astronomical coordinates and units, significantly engaging the participants.

The student session culminated in the Speaker’s Session by Dr. Ghosh titled “Astronomy: Gateway to the Cosmos”. The talk and subsequent interactive discussion enabled students to explore stellar objects and the evolution of the Universe. Following lunch, students visited the Fr. Eugene Lafont Observatory, where they were trained in telescope handling despite unfavourable weather conditions.
