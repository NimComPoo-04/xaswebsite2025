Summer School 2024: Hands On Astronomy at F.E.L.O

“Something as mere as age in the immensity of Curiosity, is but a young Sun against the entirety of the Sky.”

Hands-on Astronomy at F.E.L.O 2024, organised by the Postgraduate and Research Department of Physics in association with the Xaverian Astronomical Society, was the concluding societal event of XAS for the academic session 2023–24. The two-day summer workshop was designed for students of Classes XI and XII from St. Xavier’s Collegiate School and Don Bosco School, Bandel.

Conducted on 19th and 20th July, the workshop combined theoretical discussions, hands-on activities at the Fr. Eugene Lafont Observatory, and computational analysis of experimental data, with the aim of instilling curiosity and appreciation for Astronomy and Astrophysics among young learners.

Inauguration and Foundational Concepts

The workshop was inaugurated on 19th July at D’Souza Hall by Rev. Dr. Dominic Savio SJ, Principal of St. Xavier’s College (Autonomous), Kolkata. Following the inaugural ceremony, student-led sessions introduced the participants to the fundamental mechanics of the sky.

These sessions covered astronomical coordinates and units, providing the necessary mathematical groundwork for the telescope sessions. Dr. Sanjay Kumar Ghosh’s lecture, “Astronomy: Gateway to the Cosmos”, further expanded on these concepts, detailing stellar evolution and the lifecycle of stars.

Solar Astrophysics and Computational Analysis

The second day, 20th July, focused on our closest star—the Sun. The session began with the felicitation of Guest Speaker Dr. Tapas Kumar Das, Associate Professor at the Harish-Chandra Research Institute (HRI), Prayagraj.

Under the guidance of Dr. Das and the XAS volunteers, students engaged in computational analysis of solar data. Using recent observations from solar missions, students plotted sunspot cycles and calculated solar rotation periods. This hands-on approach allowed the participants to experience the transition from raw telescopic observation to scientific data interpretation.

The workshop concluded with a valedictory session where participants were awarded certificates. The Summer School successfully bridged the gap between school-level physics and the vast frontier of modern astrophysics, leaving the students with a renewed "fire of curiosity."
