Astrovaganza 2024

Flagship inter-disciplinary event and the Father Eugene Lafont Memorial Lecture

The Stars That Matter

The Postgraduate and Research Department of Physics, in association with the Xaverian Astronomical Society, organized a joint event: Astrovaganza '24 and the Father Eugene Lafont Memorial Lecture '24. Sponsored by mymacaw.com, the occasion featured Prof. Dibyendu Nandi from IISER Kolkata, who delivered a lecture in memory of Father Lafont. For the second part of the event, renowned astrophotographer Mr. Soumyadeep Mukherjee from Astronomads Bangla was invited to deliver an enthralling session on astronomical imaging.

On the 2nd of April, the event began at 3:15 pm in the Fr. Depelchin Auditorium. The inaugural address was delivered by Rev. Fr. Principal Dr. Dominic Savio SJ, who welcomed the guests of honor and briefed attendees on the legacy of the Xaverian Astronomical Society. Following the inauguration, Prof. Dibyendu Nandi began his intriguing lecture titled “The Stars That Matter: Lessons from the Sun, Earth and Life.” He elaborated on the critical impact of the space environment on natural habitability and provided a detailed explanation of how Earth became the only planet known to harbor life.

The Sky is the Limit

The second session, Astrovaganza '24, began at 4:40 pm with Mr. Soumyadeep Mukherjee's lecture, “The Sky is the Limit: Discussing Astrophotography.” He addressed the basic requirements of astrophotography, the technical processes involved, and the specialized equipment used for high-fidelity astronomical imaging. Throughout the event, an ongoing Astrophotography Exhibition hosted by Astronomads Bangla was held in the foyer of the Fr. Depelchin Auditorium, showcasing celestial wonders captured by local enthusiasts.

Celestial Observations

The academic sessions were followed by night sky observation events held on the 5th and 8th of April at the Fr. Eugene Lafont Observatory. Registered students were given the opportunity to observe celestial objects through the society's telescopes, bridging the gap between theoretical lectures and practical experience.

The session concluded with a vote of thanks delivered by the Secretary of the Xaverian Astronomical Society. Between the profound lecture sessions, the astounding exhibition, and the mesmerizing sky observations, the event was a grand success, further fueling the scientific curiosity of the student body.
